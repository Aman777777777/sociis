I.

The institution.

SociisGroup is an independent behavioural institution. Registered in Brussels, Belgium. Founded in 2023 by Jeremie Landweer.

The institution is governed by the Sociis Ethical Council, guided by the LIME Code, and structured around two divisions: Sociis Impact, which builds, and Sociis Culture, which scales.

SociisGroup is not a consultancy. It is not a community. It is not a tech company. It is not an NGO. It is not a certification body. It is not a think tank. It does not describe itself by what it is not. It is an independent behavioural institution.

II.

The mandate.

One billion people. One behavioural standard. By 2030.

This mandate is not a target. It is institutional intent, in motion.

The thesis: the economic system shifts only when individuals shift. One billion people behaving more sustainably is the threshold at which markets, supply chains and governance structures begin to follow. And culture moves faster than governance. Regulation takes decades. Standards emerge from committees. Culture reaches billions in years.

SociisGroup deploys culture as an institutional channel, not as a marketing tool, to carry behavioural standards at the speed the planet requires.

III.

The canonical opening line.

We serve humanity by changing how the world behaves.

This is the locked opening statement. It appears, verbatim, on the website hero. It may be quoted in outward materials. It may be adapted for a specific audience but the two verbs (serve, change) and the two objects (humanity, behaviour) must remain.

Subordinate framing lines that may accompany this opening:

  • The independent behavioural institution.
  • Humanity is taking more from this planet than it can regenerate. The problem is not knowledge. Everyone knows what needs to change. The problem is behaviour.
  • Not in training programmes. Not in reports. At the source.
IV.

The Mark.

The SociisGroup mark consists of two complete vertical lines, equal, side by side, enclosed within a single circle.

Its meaning is institutional and non-negotiable:

Two complete individuals, equal, side by side. No one above, no one below. Impact is created with people, not on top of people.

The Mark is not a logo. It is a philosophical statement. It does not hold product association. It holds ethical association.

The Mark is reserved for BEE, the Behavioural Engine of Ethics, where the philosophy it carries is rendered into a working tool. The Mark does not appear as the logo of SociisGroup on the public homepage. The institutional surface uses the SociisGroup wordmark. The Mark belongs to BEE and to internal institutional materials where its philosophical weight is appropriate to the context.

V.

The Source.

Everything starts with behaviour.

Every institution that has lost its way did so before the numbers showed it. The cause was always behavioural, because institutions are individuals, and institutions are communities. What those individuals and communities chose to prioritise, to ignore, to tolerate, and to reward.

SociisGroup intervenes where behaviour actually lives. In the individual. In the community. At the source of the decisions that shape what the rest of the economy then has to report.

Through SBS, the Sustainable Behavioural Standard, the reference frame individuals and communities conduct themselves against. Through BEE, the Behavioural Engine of Ethics, which surfaces the gap between what a person intends and what they actually produce. Through Rooms, environments where behaviour becomes visible among peers who hold the same standard.

Mindset shifts. Behaviour follows. Decisions change. Outcomes change.

SociisGroup operates upstream of ESG. Behaviour is the missing B in ESG. The layer that governs the Environmental, the Social, and the Governance long before any of them reach a disclosure framework.

VI.

The four mechanisms.

SociisGroup engages individuals and communities through four interlocking mechanisms. The four sit at the same institutional level. They build a behavioural framework today that is designed to evolve into a recognised global standard over time.

01. The Interface.

BEE, the Behavioural Engine of Ethics. A behavioural interface layer powered by AI, designed to surface the gap between judgement and actual behaviour at the level of individuals and communities. BEE is where behavioural learning begins. Every interaction contributes to the evidence base from which institutional standards are built.

02. The Rooms.

Controlled environments where leadership conduct becomes visible under pressure. Three formats: The 22 (Leadership Table), The 64 (Founding Gathering), The 300 (Immersive Environment). These are not conventional events. They are the proof environments of the institution, where behaviour is observed, tested and calibrated in person.

03. The Culture.

Film, music, art, publishing, Let There Be Light, immersive formats. Culture moves faster than governance and reaches further than boardrooms. It is the channel through which behaviour becomes visible, desirable and lived at institutional scale. This is the mechanism that makes the one billion mandate credible.

04. The Standard.

SBS, the Sustainable Behavioural Standard. The framework today and the standard in formation. Learnings from the Interface, evidence from the Rooms, and distribution through Culture converge, over time, into an ethical and behavioural reference frame against which institutional conduct is built and held. SBS sits upstream of disclosure, regulation and reporting. Its transition from framework to standard is a multi-year arc, grounded in lived behavioural proof rather than declared doctrine.

The Interface, the Rooms, the Culture and the Standard form a single behavioural infrastructure. Today a framework. Over time, a standard.

VII.

The two divisions.

SociisGroup is built around two divisions. They interlock.

Sociis Impact builds.

SBS, the Sustainable Behavioural Standard. SBS defines the ethical and behavioural reference frame for how individuals and institutions conduct themselves toward higher levels of sustainability and dignity.

BEE, the Behavioural Engine of Ethics. BEE is a behavioural interface layer powered by AI that helps individuals discover the gap between their deep values and the behaviour they are actually producing.

Sociis Culture scales.

  • Rooms. The 22, The 64, The 300. Controlled environments where behaviour becomes visible and is calibrated without public exposure.
  • House. The continuous environment where Sociis communities stay connected through conversations, signals and shared narratives.
  • Culture. Film, music, art, publishing, Let There Be Light. The cultural channels through which the Sustainable Behavioural Standard reaches the world.

There are two divisions. There are never five. There are never three. There are never more than two. Anyone who writes about SociisGroup as having more than two divisions is working from an outdated briefing.

VIII.

The three domains.

SociisGroup operates across three domains. The domains are where the work lives, not how the institution is structured.

Sustainability. The outcome.

The outcome of how individuals and communities conduct themselves toward the highest standards. The Sustainable Behavioural Standard converts intent into verifiable behavioural conduct. What institutions report is no longer sufficient. How they behave is now the standard.

Hospitality. The environments.

Controlled environments where leadership behaviour becomes visible under pressure. From institutional gatherings to global immersions, the institutions SociisGroup engages with provide the physical ground where mindset shifts toward more sustainable and dignified conduct are shaped, tested and amplified.

Humanity. The centre.

At the centre of everything. The standard exists to serve human beings, not systems. Culture and humanitarian initiatives anchor the institutional mission. Dignity is not an afterthought. It is the reason the institution exists.

IX.

The LIME Code.

The balance between Life and Time, and everything in between.

The LIME Code is the ethical and behavioural architecture governing SociisGroup. It powers SBS, shapes institutional conduct, and serves as the reference framework for the Sociis Ethical Council.

The LIME Code will assist people to change how they judge, what they consume, what they tolerate, and what they request from the world around them. It is structured in two layers.

Outer Conduct

Generosity

The LIME Code is the signature move of SociisGroup. Its language is protected. Its principles are not editable.

X.

The Sociis Ethical Council.

The Sociis Ethical Council is the institutional body that guards the LIME Code and oversees the integrity of SociisGroup as it scales.

Its mandate:

  • Guard the LIME Code.
  • Review how SBS is defined and governed.
  • Oversee data ethics for Sociis House and BEE.
  • Question any collaboration, Room or conduct that drifts from institutional standards.

The Ethical Council does not hold legal authority over SociisGroup. Its power is moral and institutional. The strength of the Council lies in the quality of the people who sit on it, and in the seriousness with which the institution listens.

The LIME Code ensures institutional integrity as SociisGroup scales.

By 2030, one billion people will share a new behavioural standard based on the LIME Code principles.

XI.

The product is proximity. A seat in the Room. A table in the Room. Access to the Room. The Room itself belongs to the institution.

XI.b

Annual Participation.

SociisGroup operates on three annual participation tiers, distinct from Room access. Annual tiers are how individuals and institutions stand with SociisGroup over time, beyond the seat or table they take in a single Room.

Sovereign Partners.

€150,000 annual, excl. VAT. Cap of eight institutions. Apex tier. Sovereign Partners are the flag holders. They co-develop SBS at the frame level. They carry the standard into their own institutions and across the network. Seat on the Sovereign Assembly. Permanent naming on the institutional record. Visibility at every Room, every city, every year. By direct invitation of the Founder.

Founding Partners.

€75,000 annual, excl. VAT. Cap of one per category, four categories. Annual category-chair tier. Founding Partners carry visible category leadership across the annual Rooms programme. Renewable each year, same institution or new. The four categories are Capital, Media, Technology, Mobility. By direct invitation of the Founder.

Circle Members.

€15,000 annual, excl. VAT. Cap of twenty-two individuals. Annual individual tier. Circle Members are Practitioners. They use BEE. They sit in the Rooms. Their conduct produces the behavioural evidence that feeds SBS as it forms into a standard. Access to all Rooms across every location. Introductions to peers, Founding Partners, and Sovereign Partners. By invitation or direct enquiry.

Three annual tiers. Three Room formats. One institution. The annual tiers fund and shape the institution. The Rooms are where its work becomes visible.

Commercial vocabulary.

The annual tiers are not memberships. They are participation tiers. A Sovereign Partner does not "join" SociisGroup. A Sovereign Partner stands with the institution as a flag holder for the year. Same logic applies to Founding Partners and Circle Members. Language: contribute, stand with, participate as. Never join, buy, subscribe, sponsor.

XIII.

Voice and register.

SociisGroup speaks in one voice across all surfaces: calm, declarative, institutional.

  • Short sentences. Complete thoughts.
  • No marketing softeners. No we believe. No we help. No we are passionate about.
  • No hedging. No qualifiers that weaken. No modal verbs that evade.
  • Paragraphs are short, typically two to four sentences. White space is institutional.
  • Separators are commas or full stops. Never dashes.
  • Prices are stated directly as excl. VAT, without asterisks or footnote indirection.
  • Numbers are declarative, never tentative. "One billion people will share" not "there will be one billion individuals sharing."
  • The institution does not perform. The institution states.

XV.

Forbidden vocabulary.

Certain terms are forbidden in outward materials. They dilute the institutional register or collapse SociisGroup into the wrong category. Do not use.

  • Decisions. Decision-making. Decision support. Decision quality. SociisGroup works upstream of decisions, at the level of behaviour. Any use of decision language collapses SociisGroup into the consultancy category.
  • Partnership. Always collaboration, except in the locked tier names Sovereign Partners and Founding Partners.
  • Experiences. Always Rooms.
  • Trusted Advisor or Trusted Advisors. Always Contributors, or name the specific role.
  • Sovereign as a standalone word in openings. Reserved for internal speeches by Jeremie. The locked term Sovereign Partners is permitted as the apex tier name.
  • Ecosystem. Use environment, or name the specific thing.
  • ESG as a positioning. SociisGroup sits upstream of ESG. The framing "the missing B in ESG" is permitted only as positioning, never as category.
  • Consultancy. NGO. Certification body. Tech company. SociisGroup is none of these. Do not define SociisGroup by what it is not.
  • The sustainability sector. The behavioural change sector. SociisGroup is not in any sector. SociisGroup is an independent institution.
  • Higher standard. The locked phrase is more sustainable standard. Not higher.
  • SIBI. Internal reference only, never outward.
  • The Sacred Sequence. Internal only, never outward.
  • Pie Jesu. Internal only, never named outward. The unannounced closing of The 64.
  • Private behavioural institution. Always independent.
  • At Tomorrowland (unqualified). Always the full framing.
  • Tomorrowland as a brand SociisGroup collaborates with. The collaboration is with Love Tomorrow. The grounds are the iconic grounds of Tomorrowland.
  • Founding Tables €30,000 cap of four. Old commercial framing. Replaced by the 48-Hour Arc Table at €30,000 with no cap stated on the public surface.
  • Testers for Circle Members. The locked term is Practitioners.
  • Sponsor. Sponsorship. Annual tiers are participation, not sponsorship. Use contribute, stand with, participate as.
  • Member, Membership. Permitted only for Circle Members specifically. Do not call Sovereign or Founding Partners members.
  • Join. Reader does not join SociisGroup. Reader belongs to SociisGroup the moment their conduct is already the standard. Reader takes a seat, takes a table, stands with the institution.
  • Dashes as separators. Always commas or full stops.
  • Asterisks on prices. Always state excl. VAT directly.
  • Marketing softeners. We believe. We help. We are passionate about.
  • Buyer-language softeners on the public homepage. Buy. Purchase. Subscription. Customer.
  • Benefit corporation. B Corp. Belgium has no such legal designation. Using either creates due diligence risk. Forbidden on outward materials.
