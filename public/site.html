Everything starts with behaviour.

Humanity is taking more from this planet than it can regenerate. The problem is not knowledge. Everyone knows what needs to change. The problem is behaviour.

Every institution that has lost its way did so before the numbers showed it. The cause was always behavioural. What people chose to prioritise, to ignore, to tolerate, and to reward.

SociisGroup intervenes at the source.

Through SBS, the Sustainable Behavioural Standard, which defines the ethical and behavioural reference frame for how institutions conduct themselves. How SociisGroup Works

Four mechanisms.
One behavioural infrastructure.

In practice, SociisGroup engages individuals and communities through four interlocking mechanisms. Together, they build a behavioural framework today that is designed to evolve into a recognised global standard over time.

We want to change the behaviour of one billion individuals for higher sustainability standards. We use the code of culture to get there, because culture moves much faster than governance.

Two convictions set SociisGroup apart from every institution in the sustainability space. First, the economic system shifts only when individuals shift. One billion people behaving more sustainably is the threshold at which markets, supply chains and governance structures begin to follow. Second, culture moves faster than governance. Regulation takes decades. Standards emerge from committees. Culture reaches billions in years.

Governance and Integrity

The LIME Code.

The balance between Life and Time, and everything in between.

The LIME Code is the ethical and behavioural architecture governing SociisGroup. It powers SBS, shapes institutional conduct, and serves as the reference framework for the Sociis Ethical Council.

The LIME Code will assist people to change how they judge, what they consume, what they tolerate, and what they request from the world around them. It is structured in two layers. Four principles govern inner conduct under pressure. Four principles govern how power is exercised in relation to others and institutions.

Inner Conduct
Clarity
Focus before movement.
Integrity
Alignment when unseen.
Restraint
Grace over impulse.
Courage
The hard right over the easy wrong.
Outer Conduct
Discernment
Wisdom before speed.
Generosity
Influence shared.
Presence
Composure under pressure.
Remembrance
Humanity before numbers.
Governance

The Sociis Ethical Council.

  • Guard the LIME Code.
  • Review how SBS is defined and governed.
  • Oversee data ethics for Sociis House and BEE.
  • Question any collaboration, Room or conduct that drifts from institutional standards.

The Ethical Council does not hold legal authority over SociisGroup. Its power is moral and institutional. The strength of the Council lies in the quality of the people who sit on it, and in the seriousness with which the institution listens.

The LIME Code ensures institutional integrity as SociisGroup scales.

By 2030, one billion people will share a new behavioural standard based on the LIME Code principles.
